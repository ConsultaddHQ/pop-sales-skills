# Reference Case: Regina — Thermal Ledger

This is the fourth canonical case, and the FIRST one representing a clean positive outcome (she was sold before the scope doc existed). Use this as a reference for what the closing motion looks like, not just the research.

## Prospect archetype: Non-technical founder needing technical partner

Distinct from the other three archetypes:
- Brian — solo operator with existing business, needs marketing services
- Mauro — B2B tech buyer inside established company
- Scott — technical founder, pivoted to partnership
- **Regina — non-technical founder, pre-product, needs builder + fractional CTO**

Signals for this archetype:
- Co-founder team with domain expertise (construction, legal, operations) but no technical capability
- Pre-product, pre-revenue, bootstrap-funded
- Has customer demand waiting on product
- Was about to hire engineer or give equity to a technical cofounder
- Small initial budget ($5-20k range) but open to raising F&F for more
- Wants a partner, not a vendor

## The Thermal Ledger deal history

- **Outbound LinkedIn touch:** Bharat reached out to Regina. Timing was perfect — she was days away from posting a backend developer job on Indeed and was debating bringing on a third technical cofounder with equity.
- **First call (Apr 23, 2026):** 30-ish minute call. Outcome: Regina said "best case scenario here." Agreed to send scope doc. Follow-up scheduled for Wednesday 1:15 PM.

## TL;DR

Regina is a law student (finishing this year, bar exam July) who is co-founding Thermal Ledger with Scott, a facilities/HVAC veteran with strong Boston building-owner relationships. They have a zero-to-one IoT product idea (environmental sensors → dashboard → auto-ticketing) and customers already waiting to buy, but NO technical capability on the founding team. They were about to post a job on Indeed or give equity to a third technical cofounder. **This is a fractional-CTO / discovery-sprint sale, not a SaaS sale.** Right entry: $3-5k discovery sprint usable as credit toward Phase 2 build. Position Bharat as acting CTO who will defend the technical platform in future fundraising meetings. Avoid generic content/marketing pitches — irrelevant to their pain.

- **Tech stack:** One building already has Ulta sensors, manual review currently, no dashboard/automation yet
- **Location:** Regina (likely Wisconsin/Midwest), Scott (Boston)

## Hidden Differentiator

**Scott's Boston network is the untapped distribution asset.** They don't need to find customers; customers are waiting. The bottleneck is product, not go-to-market. Most zero-to-one founders don't have this asset — they have a product idea and need to find customers. Regina/Scott have the inverse problem. This changes what "success" looks like: ship a working MVP fast, because customers convert on delivery, not on sales motion.

## Vulnerability Signals (why now)

- **They were about to hire on Indeed.** The hiring decision is being made THIS WEEK. If Bharat doesn't land, someone from the job board does.
- **Equity-cofounder conversation was active.** They were willing to give away equity for technical capability. That means a non-equity alternative (partnership, fractional CTO) has massive value.
- **Customers waiting erodes if delayed.** Each week without product, Scott's Boston contacts cool. Urgency is real.
- **Bar exam in July limits Regina's bandwidth.** After July she's also managing a bar-prep sprint. Product needs momentum before then.

**5 Whys applied:**
- Surface: "We need a backend developer."
- Why? Zero to one product needs engineering we don't have.
- Why does that matter? Without product, customers can't buy.
- Why does that matter? Scott's network goes cold if we don't ship.
- Why does that matter? We lose the first-mover advantage in his network.
- **Core:** The asset is Scott's network. The risk is losing it. Every week matters. They'll pay for speed.

| Offering | Fit | Evidence |

| Growth plan ($495) | **LOW** | Same. |
| AI Voice Agent | **LOW** | Not relevant to IoT dashboard build. |
| **Custom AI / product build** | **HIGHEST** | IoT dashboard is exactly this. |

**Recommended entry:** $3-5k discovery sprint → scoped MVP build in phases → Bharat acts as fractional CTO attending their fundraising meetings. Zero equity required. Milestone billing.

## Do NOT Pitch

- Generic content marketing / social media services — irrelevant
- High upfront fees or all-in packages — she has $10k from personal savings
- Equity engagement — she wanted to AVOID equity cost of a third cofounder; don't reintroduce it
- Pure vendor/contractor positioning — she'll prefer Indeed hire. Lead with partner framing.

## Discovery Questions

Grounded in research:

1. "You mentioned a third cofounder with equity was on the table. What made you hesitate on that path?"
2. "Scott has the Boston building-owner network. How many of those contacts are actively waiting for the product vs. interested but not yet committed?"
3. "You have one building already on sensors with manual review. How painful is that manual review today, and who does it?"
4. "Bar exam in July — how do you want the product timeline to map against that?"
5. "What's the metric you'd use to know the MVP was ready enough to launch with first paying customer?"
6. "Have you had conversations with VCs yet, or is this friends & family first?"
7. "What sensor vendors do you want to support beyond Ulta?"

## What this case teaches the skill

1. **New archetype: non-technical founder pre-product.** Add to the profile classification. Signals: zero-to-one, bootstrap budget, hiring or considering equity cofounder, customer demand waiting on product.

2. **The close is as important as the discovery.** For this archetype, the right move isn't "pitch services" — it's "position as fractional CTO + discovery sprint as credit." The skill should surface the closing pattern, not just the research.

3. **Watch for equity-alternative framing.** When a prospect is considering giving equity for technical capability, the right pitch is "fractional CTO without equity cost." Same value, better terms.

4. **Timing signals matter.** Regina was days from posting on Indeed. The skill should surface hiring-decision signals (job posts, "we're looking for," "about to hire") as vulnerability markers — they mean the buying window is THIS week.

5. **Distribution-is-the-asset prospects.** When the prospect has customer demand waiting on product (vs. product waiting on customers), urgency is inverted. Speed-to-ship beats feature completeness. The skill should recognize and flag this.
