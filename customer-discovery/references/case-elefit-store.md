# Reference Case: Sudheera — The EleFit Store (Hiring-Inflection Gate)

Short case, focused on the ONE pattern this call teaches that the others didn't.

## Prospect snapshot

- **Company:** The EleFit Store (sports/athletics gear — cooling towels, duffel bags, etc.)
- **Contact:** Sudheera (founder)
- **Stack:** Shopify + Amazon (India thriving, US competitive), Groq for AI, own dev team (Pranav is lead)
- **Pipeline position:** AI coach launching THIS WEEK (ships 2026-04-28 ish)
- **Profile:** Founder-led SMB with existing tech stack
- **Qualification:** 4/5 signals (quantified pain, failing workaround, revenue target/timeline, founder doing work, proactive ✓)

## The call (2026-04-24)

Anushka demoed the AEO/SEO tool. Sudheera engaged well, asked good questions, said "this all looks great." Bharat asked the qualifying question: "is this something your business needs?"

Her answer was the whole deal:

> "We are struggling with ranking a little bit. Our team is much bigger starting May 1st. We hired a few SEO specialists because we wanted to ramp that task up. I'm also seeing some value in this, so I would like to explore more. I also want to take the opinions of the people you have hired as well... I could not ask them to join before I hired them. I have to wait for the next months to begin when they get on board, and I will definitely be looking at this once they are ready."

## The pattern (NEW — add to skill)

**Hiring-inflection decision gate.** The prospect is in a hiring window. New team starts May 1st. Decision is frozen until then. AND the new hires already have tools in mind — "they also spoke about some tools that are existing that they will be using." Those tools are now your default competition.

### Why this is different from "not ready yet"

"Not ready yet" = prospect is testing their own process, needs to feel the pain more. Wait.

"Hiring-inflection gate" = prospect HAS the pain, HAS the budget authority, but the decision committee doesn't exist yet. Waiting means losing to whatever tools the new hires prefer.

### The counter-move

**Pre-audit delivered BEFORE the new team arrives.** Specifically:

1. Offer to run a 5-7 day audit (ranking analysis, content gap analysis, LLM presence check — whatever fits the offering) between now and when the new team starts.
2. Deliver the audit 1-2 days before the new team's first day.
3. When the new team starts, they walk in to a document with data about their own business — not a vendor pitch deck.
4. You become the advisor they turn to, not the vendor they evaluate.

This flips the asymmetry. Without the audit, you're competing with their tools of choice. With the audit, you ARE their starting point.

### Signal words to listen for

- "Starting May 1st" / "new team joining" / "just hired" / "onboarding"
- "Once they're ready" / "when they get on board" / "I'll loop them in"
- "They have tools they'll be using" / "they'll evaluate options"
- "I want their opinion" / "I'll have to ask them"

When 2+ of these appear in a single call, classify the deal as HIRING-INFLECTION and trigger the pre-audit counter-move.

## What the /customer-discovery skill should flag pre-call

If LinkedIn shows the prospect posted a hiring announcement in the last 30 days (or the team LinkedIn shows new hires starting soon), surface it in the Vulnerability Signals section as:

> "🚨 Hiring inflection: {Company} is onboarding {role} in {timeframe}. Decision will likely be gated until arrival. Recommend pre-audit counter-move before deal goes cold."

## Followup for THIS deal (actionable)

- Lock follow-up meeting NOW (before "early next week" becomes "next month")
- Ask who on the new SEO team should be on next call
- Offer the pre-audit between now and May 1
- Tease the chat tool as reason to meet again (Bharat promised to walk through it)
- Ask what tools the new hires plan to use — learn the competition for free
