# Pop Sales Skills — Cheat Sheet

Print this. Pin it next to your monitor.

---

## The 5 commands you actually use

```
claude                                                  # open Claude Code

/customer-discovery "Name" "Company" "Location"        # pre-call research (NEW prospects)

/meeting-prep "Company"                                # pre-call prep (ONGOING deals, meeting #2+)

/meeting-debrief                                        # post-call recap + follow-up email

/pop-one-pager "Company"                                # generate proposal
```

That's it. Everything else stacks on top of these.

**When to use which:**
- New prospect, first call → `/customer-discovery`
- Same prospect, second call onward → `/meeting-prep` (reads full history)
- Right after any call → `/meeting-debrief`

---

## The 5 qualification signals (memorize)

Strong prospects have ≥3 of these. Weak prospects have ≤1.

1. **Quantified pain** — they name numbers (hours, dollars, percentages)
2. **Failing workaround** — they tried something, it broke
3. **Revenue target + timeline** — "$X by date Y"
4. **Founder doing the work** — pain is felt, not delegated
5. **Proactive reach-out** — they engaged fast, asked smart questions

---

## The 5 objections you'll hear (and the moves)

| Objection | Move |
|---|---|
| "AI can't replace human connection" | Acknowledge + reframe as operational support behind the relationship |
| "Not ready / testing first" | Validate timing, schedule follow-up, don't push |
| "Cost / ROI uncertain" | Discovery sprint as low-risk entry ($3-5k refundable) |
| "I can tell it's AI" (post-demo) | Customization + scale advantage |
| "Need proof / references" | Case studies + tailored scope doc |

---

## The 6 prospect profiles

| Profile | Right move |
|---|---|
| Solo operator / small biz | Starter plan, content/marketing-led |
| Non-technical founder pre-product | Fractional CTO + discovery sprint |
| Founder-led SMB with tech stack | Per-workflow pricing, integration-led |
| Technical founder | Custom or partnership, prove engineering |
| Government / RFP / Enterprise | Pilot → champion → contract |
| **POOR FIT** | Don't pursue. Move on. |

---

## When to disqualify (don't waste time)

- **Relationship-core business** — their value IS the human relationship (MLM, coaching)
- **No product, no channel** — pre-launch with no customers yet
- **Lifestyle business, no growth ambition** — comfortable with current limits
- **Just exploring, no budget** — vague curiosity, no specific pain
- **Wrong-time personal circumstance** — major life event, business sale in progress

The skill flags these in the brief. Trust it.

---

## Vulnerability signals (why NOW)

Look for these in research:

- 🔴 **Personnel changes** — losing a key person, leadership shift
- 🔴 **Hiring inflection** — new team starts in <30 days, will gate decisions
- 🔴 **Build-vs-buy pivot** — moving from internal tools to vendors
- 🔴 **ROI clock ticking** — recent platform investment, needs to justify
- 🔴 **Distribution-is-the-asset** — customer demand waiting on product
- 🔴 **Founder doing manual work** — pain felt at the top

## The default close move

> "Discovery sprint, $3-5k, refundable as credit toward Phase 2 build. Milestone-based billing. We deliver in 5-7 days."

Use when prospect is solo operator, non-technical founder, or founder-led SMB with budget under ~$20k. Don't use for government/RFP, technical-founder partnerships, or disqualified prospects.

---

## The hiring-inflection counter-move

When prospect says "I'll loop in my new team starting May 1st" or similar:

> "Between now and {start date}, we run a {5-7 day} audit on {their domain}. Output is a document for your new team to use day-1 — not a vendor pitch deck. We absorb the audit cost as part of getting to know your business."

This puts you in the advisor seat before their new tools arrive.

---

## Email rules

- Subject line: specific, references the call (60 chars max)
- Opening: reference a specific moment from the call (not "great meeting")
- Action items: split by side, with deadlines
- Value-add: a real insight or link, not "AI is transforming industries"
- Next meeting: confirmed with date + time + timezone
- Total length: ≤8 sentences + action items

---

## When in doubt

1. Run the skill. Don't manually research.
2. If the brief is wrong, tell us. Skills improve with feedback.
3. If you're stuck, ask in #pop-skills. Someone has hit this before.
4. If a prospect is clearly disqualified, walk away. Time is the asset.
