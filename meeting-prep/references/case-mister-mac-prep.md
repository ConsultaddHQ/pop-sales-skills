## What this case teaches the skill

1. **Open commitments are the meeting's primary risk surface.** Surface them at the top, flag overdue items aggressively, give the AE the exact opening line ("Promised this on April 17, here it is, sorry for the lag.").

2. **The deal stage framework breaks for partnerships.** Don't force "discovery → demo → proposal → close" on a deal that became co-building. Use the partnership lens: balanced commitments, first specific opportunity, agreed structure.

3. **Profile reclassification matters.** Scott started as "technical founder customer" and is now "partner opportunity." Marking the reclassification + date helps the AE understand WHY the playbook changed.

4. **Predicted questions should reference specific prior moments.** Not "what's their use case?" — instead "He WILL ask if you sent the video. He committed to evaluate it. That commitment is overdue from us, not them."

5. **Hidden differentiator continuity check.** Was the original insight validated by the deal's shape? In Scott's case, yes — the original differentiator (human-first moat) drove the entire pivot. Reuse it.
