# Email Examples: Good vs Bad Follow-up Emails

Use these as the bar when /meeting-debrief drafts the follow-up. The difference between "great meeting, looking forward" and a real follow-up is the difference between a deal that stalls and a deal that moves.

## Example 1: Regina / Thermal Ledger (positive outcome)

### ❌ BAD (generic)

```
Subject: Follow-up

Hi Regina,

Thanks so much for taking the time to chat today! It was great learning
about Thermal Ledger. I'll follow up with next steps soon.

Let me know if you have any questions in the meantime.

Best,
Bharat
```

**Why it's bad:** Generic subject, zero specific references, "next steps soon" is fuzzy, no action items, no value-add, no next meeting confirmation.

### ✓ GOOD (grounded in the call)

```
Subject: Thermal Ledger scope doc + Wednesday 1:15 PM

Hi Regina,

Great timing on your LinkedIn — I was about to post that Indeed job too,
so I know exactly where you are. The fact that Scott already has Boston
building owners waiting makes this a speed-to-ship problem, not a
find-customers problem. That's a good position to be in.

**From you this week:**
- Send the MVP scope doc (sensor types, dashboard requirements, threshold
  rules) to bharat@teampop.com by Monday

**From us by next meeting:**
- Scope document with discovery sprint details
- Floor-and-ceiling pricing
- Example architecture for IoT + dashboard + auto-ticketing

One thing worth a look before we meet: Particle.io and Losant both
have IoT-to-dashboard frameworks that might save us months of plumbing
work. I'll include both in the scope doc.

Wednesday, April 30, 1:15 PM ET — calendar invite coming separately.

Talk then,
Bharat
```

**Why it's good:**
- Subject specific: scope doc + specific meeting time
- Opening references EXACT moment (timing on LinkedIn, Scott's Boston network)
- Reframes the problem in HER favor (speed-to-ship, not find-customers)
- Action items split by side, with deadlines
- Value-add: specific tools she should know about (Particle.io, Losant) — shows expertise, saves her research
- Meeting confirmed with timezone
- Short (4 bullets + 3 short paragraphs)

## Example 2: Sudheera / EleFit Store (hiring-inflection gate)

### ❌ BAD (generic)

```
Subject: Following up

Hi Sudheera,

Great demo today! We'd love to work with you. Let me know when you've
spoken to your team and we can take it from there.

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Bharat
```

**Why it's bad:** Passive, waits for her, loses the deal while she waits 2 months for her team to start. Doesn't address the hiring-inflection gate.

### ✓ GOOD (with pre-audit counter-move)

```
Subject: EleFit audit + early May check-in

Hi Sudheera,

Your India traction is what stood out today — Amazon India excelling while
the US is a knife fight is a market signal most people in your category
don't see yet. That's worth leaning into.

Here's what I'm thinking, given your new SEO team starts May 1st:

**From us, between now and May 1:**
- 5-7 day ranking audit across your Shopify + Amazon US + Amazon India
  listings, measuring presence in Google AI Overview, ChatGPT, Perplexity,
  and Gemini for your top 20 product keywords
- Output: a document your new SEO team can use as day-1 data (not a sales
  deck). We cover the audit as part of getting to know your business.

**From you:**
- Quick yes/no on whether we can run the audit
- A note to your incoming SEO lead that there's a working doc waiting for
  them, if they want to use it

**Early next week (before May 1):** I'll walk you through the chat tool
we held back today — it's more relevant to your AI coach launch than
the SEO tool, honestly. Proposing Wednesday, April 30 at {same time}.

The goal is you have data ready when your team arrives. Not selling
against whatever tools they bring. Selling WITH them.

Bharat
```

**Why it's good:**
- Subject names the counter-move (audit) + meeting
- Opening validates her specific strategic insight (India vs US)
- Pre-audit counter-move is specific and scoped
- Split by side with clear actions
- Explicitly addresses the hiring-inflection fear ("not selling against your team")
- Teases the chat tool (the promised hook)
- Short, concrete, founder-to-founder tone

## Example 3: Tanner / Amway (soft no, graceful exit)

### ✓ GOOD (short, leaves door open)

```
Subject: Appreciate the conversation, Tanner

Hi Tanner,

You were right — 99% of your business is the human connection, and AI
can't touch that part. Not today, probably not next year either. Pitching
you on automation over that would have been a waste of your time.

If you ever run into a repeatable, pattern-based operational task — the
kind you'd love to stop doing yourself — shoot me a note. Not selling,
just curious.

Best,
Bharat
```

**Why it's good:**
- Acknowledges his point verbatim
- Doesn't push, doesn't sell
- Leaves a specific re-engagement trigger ("repeatable, pattern-based task")
- Short and gracious

## Rules encoded in these examples

1. **Subject line is specific.** References the call or the next step. Never "Following up" or "Quick question."

2. **Opening sentence references a specific moment.** Something they said, a number they quoted, a detail from their business. Not "great meeting."

3. **Action items are split by side.** "From us" and "From you" with deadlines. Never "I'll follow up with next steps."

4. **Value-add is specific.** A tool name, a stat, an insight, a link that THEY would find useful. Not "AI is transforming industries."

5. **Next meeting is confirmed with date + time + timezone.** Not "let's catch up soon."

6. **Length: 4-8 sentences of prose + action items.** Anything longer loses the reader.

7. **Match the deal stage.** Advancing deals get specific next steps. Stalled deals get the counter-move. Soft-no deals get a graceful exit with a re-engagement trigger.
